What is Networking?

Networking is defined as "the construction, design ans use of a computer netowrk, including the physical (cabling, hub, switch, router, server, and so forth)." Understanding this, we have focused the Networking portion of our club to preparing students for career readyness. Members will be taught the basics of setting up LANs, WLANs, personal servers, cloud devices, router configurations, an introduction to CISCO switches and many more topics!

What's New??

Recently, Our ACM Networking team went to a regional contest in Rochester NY. The contest was designed so that members had 24 hours to setup and design an interactive minecraft server given no prior knowlege or experience. Alfred State took 2nd out of a whopping 37 teams that competed.

The ACM team also got a taste of real world experience when they went to Alfred-Almond central schools and helped setup the schools wireless netowrk. They spend the better portion of a week working closely and assisting the IT professionals at the school tasked with installing countless routers, server hubs, switches firewalls and LANS.

On December 30th, our entire ACM club will be flying out to Orlando Florida to assist in repairing the computer necesssities destroyed by the recent hurricanes. We will be staying just south of Miami and driving to the affected areas every day. We will be assisting the local networking/cable companies restore internet access to several thousand people while they recover and recouperate from the terrible disaster that struck them this year.
